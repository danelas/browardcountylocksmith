Keys Locked in the Car? Don't Break a Window

Published June 13, 2026

First Steps: Stay Calm and Assess the Situation

You close the door, hear the lock click, and see your keys sitting on the seat. Heart sinks. It's frustrating, but breaking a window or forcing a door creates hundreds in damage and still leaves you without a working key.

Before anything else: check every door and the trunk. Some vehicles auto-lock the driver door but leave others open. Check if a rear hatch or trunk release is accessible from outside — a few models let you fold rear seats from the trunk to reach the cabin.

If you have a spare key at home or with a family member, and you're not in an emergency, that's the cheapest route. If you're stranded, late for work, or a child or pet is inside, call a licensed mobile locksmith immediately. A professional can open most cars in minutes without scratching paint or damaging locks.

Why DIY Methods Often Backfire

The internet is full of coat-hanger and slim-jim videos. Reality: modern cars have side-impact door beams and anti-theft rods inside the door panel. Shoving metal or plastic down the window gap can snap linkage rods, scratch glass, or trigger airbag sensors. Repair bills run $200–$600.

Inflatable wedge kits sold online work only if you know exactly where the unlock button or handle is and have a long reach tool. Most people create a gap too wide, bend the door frame, or pop weatherstripping loose. Once the seal is compromised, wind noise and water leaks follow.

Even if you succeed, you're still locked out next time — and you've now damaged the car. Professional automotive locksmiths carry non-marring wedges, specialized reach tools, and the experience to work on hundreds of makes and models without a scratch.

What a Mobile Locksmith Does (and Why It's Faster)

A mobile automotive locksmith arrives at your location — parking lot, driveway, roadside — with tools designed for modern vehicles. Most lockouts take 5–15 minutes once on-site, depending on the car's make and anti-theft features.

For mechanical locks, the locksmith uses an inflatable wedge to create a small gap at the top of the door frame, then inserts a long reach tool to press the unlock button or pull the interior handle. The wedge is removed, the door closes normally, and you're back in.

For cars with transponder or chip keys, the locksmith can also cut and program a new key on the spot if your only key is inside and you don't have a spare. This combines lockout service with key duplication — you drive away with a working key and a backup.

Calling a locksmith is almost always cheaper than a tow to the dealer, faster than waiting for roadside assistance (which may sub-contract to a locksmith anyway), and avoids the risk of self-inflicted damage. You can explore more locksmith services we offer across Broward County.

When to Call 911 vs. a Locksmith

If a child, pet, or vulnerable person is locked inside and the weather is hot, call 911 first. Heat stroke can occur in minutes. Emergency responders are authorized to break a window if necessary.

If the person inside is safe and calm, or if you're simply locked out with no one inside, a locksmith is the right call. Police and fire departments often defer non-emergency lockouts to licensed locksmiths because they lack the tools for damage-free entry and don't carry key-programming equipment.

Many drivers assume roadside assistance will be free. Check your policy: some cover lockouts, others charge $50–$150, and most have 45–90 minute wait times. A direct call to a 24/7 mobile locksmith often gets you back in faster and with up-front pricing.

Preventing Future Lockouts

Once you've been locked out, you never want to repeat it. The simplest fix: make a spare key and keep it somewhere safe — wallet, purse, or a magnetic key box under the car (choose a discreet spot away from obvious frame rails).

Modern key fobs with push-button start require programming, so a hardware-store key blank won't start the engine. Have a locksmith or dealer cut and program a true spare. The cost is $75–$250 depending on the vehicle, far less than repeated lockout calls.

Some drivers use a lockbox with a code, mounted under the rear bumper or inside a wheel well. Just don't tape a key under the car in plain sight — thieves check there first. A few smart locks and apps let you unlock via phone if the battery dies, but these are model-specific and require installation.

If you tend to lock keys in the trunk, ask a locksmith if your car can be re-keyed so the trunk and doors use separate keys. You'll keep the door key on your person and leave the trunk key in the glovebox.

Cost of Car Lockout Service in Broward County

Prices vary by time of day, vehicle complexity, and whether you need a new key cut. Expect $75–$150 for a standard lockout during business hours. After-hours, weekends, and holidays may add $25–$50. If the locksmith must cut and program a chip key, add $100–$200 depending on make and model.

Transparent locksmiths quote the price before dispatch and don't tack on trip fees or hidden charges once on-site. Always ask for a phone quote that includes labor, parts, and any after-hours premiums.

Compare that to a dealership tow ($100–$200) plus dealer service fees ($150–$300), or the cost of replacing a broken window ($250–$600 installed). A licensed mobile locksmith is the most cost-effective option for a damage-free resolution.

Frequently Asked Questions

How long does it take a locksmith to unlock a car?

Most car lockouts take 5–15 minutes once the locksmith arrives, depending on the vehicle's make, model, and door design. Modern cars with electronic locks may take slightly longer.

Will a locksmith damage my car opening the door?

A licensed automotive locksmith uses non-marring wedges and specialized tools designed to open doors without scratching paint, bending frames, or breaking windows. Damage is extremely rare when a professional does the work.

Can a locksmith make a new key if mine is locked inside?

Yes. Mobile locksmiths can cut and program a new transponder or chip key on-site for most makes and models, so you leave with both access to your car and a working spare key.

Is it cheaper to call a locksmith or use roadside assistance?

It depends on your policy. Some roadside plans cover lockouts at no extra charge but have long wait times. Direct locksmith calls often cost $75–$150 and get you back in faster with transparent pricing.

What should I do if a child or pet is locked in the car?

Call 911 immediately if the weather is hot or the person or animal is in distress. Heat stroke can occur in minutes. If everyone is safe and calm, a locksmith is the best non-emergency option.
